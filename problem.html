The challenge of a global pandemic

HowCrowded is working towards handling one of the challenges that comes with the COVID-19 pandemic:
- Managing to keep distance to other people in order to prevent the disease to spread.
Today’s global pandemic has taught us many things. It’s made us more aware to wash our hands and perhaps most importantly to keep distance from each other. WHO (n.d.) states that to keep yourself and others safe from COVID-19 it’s important to keep a distance of at least one meter from others, and an even greater distance when one is indoors. The further away, the better. However, it is not always easy to be able to maintain distance to other people. Doing necessary chores such as going to a grocery store can make it difficult to keep distance to the other shoppers if there are too many people there at the same time. This is also a big challenge for indoor spaces that are open to the public. They need regulation to ensure that there are not too many people inside the room at the same time: be it a library, grocery store or a gym.


The challenge illustrated

These days many stores and other public spaces have a display of how many people are allowed into the store, occasionally accompanied by member of staff that checks the numbers. However, this is often only displayed by a simple note outside. For example for bigger grocery stores where the number might be up to 50 people it is difficult to see how many people are actually in the store. This put both the customer and the organization’s staff at risk of having too many people inside the same space at the same time.

Ensure healthy lives and promote well being for all at all ages

HowCrowded’s work is inspired by the UN’s sustainable goal number 3:
Ensuring healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.
The UN reports that the implications from the pandemic could potentially set back decades of improvement in healthcare and they are expecting hundred of thousands additional under-5 deaths (UN, n.d.). Already, childhood immunization programmes in around 70 countries have been interrupted (UN, n.d.). With other words, the pandemic is threatening the health outcomes and could cause massive setbacks to the overall health services provided in the world. The UN(n.d.) calls out for comprehensive health strategies and increased spending on health systems.
